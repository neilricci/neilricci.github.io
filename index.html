Neil Ricci

I make websites. I've been designing and coding for five years.

My first coding experience was with Processing, a Java library. I fell in love with the amazing community behind Processing, such an interesting mix of artistic and software nerds. I hacked into the Microsoft Kinect and made interactive art works. The infared technology of the old Microsoft Kinect is behind many of the amazing capabilities of the current iPhone 10.

Why do I focus on user‑friendly experiences? Because I've been a customer support representative.

I have worked as some form of a support agent for tech companies large (Square and Lumosity) and small (you have never heard of them). I have always loved helping people use the power of the web to empower themselves. I feel front-end development is often overlooked causing users to feel frustrated support to feel overwhelmed. Clean design is a win-win for a company and its users.

So, enough tech talk. What about interests outside of web development?

I love learning about everything transportation related. 28.5 percent of 2016 greenhouse gas emissions ( EPA.gov), the largest sector, came from transportation. Just because we invented the car does not mean we have to put everything into it. I'm interested in alternative transportation methods, from realistic and easy-to-implement bike share systems to imaginary neighborhood methods like Walt Disney's dream of The Peoplemover. Also, I am a total roller coaster 🎢 fanatic.
